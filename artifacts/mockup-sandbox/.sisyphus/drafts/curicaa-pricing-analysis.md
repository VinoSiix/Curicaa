## V. THE PROBLEM: YOU'RE LEAVING MASSIVE MONEY ON THE TABLE

### Evidence You're Underpriced

1. **Parents pay $159/yr for IXL** — and IXL is just a practice tool, not a complete curriculum. Parents pay MORE for LESS.

2. **Time4Learning has 100K+ students at $300+/yr per child** — proving the market accepts $300+ for online curriculum.

3. **Power Homeschool had 100K+ students even at $720-$1,188/yr** — parents will pay $1,000+ for complete online curriculum.

4. **The HSLDA average is $300-$600/child/year** — your $129 covers ALL children. A 2-child family gets $600-$1,200 of perceived value for $129.

5. **ESA families have $5,000-$10,000 to spend** — at $129 you're not even a rounding error. They'd happily pay $249 or $349.

6. **Your own "40% off" banner implies the real price is $215** — you've already signaled you know $129 is a discount.

### The "Too Cheap" Risk

From the parent research:
> Parents associate price with quality. At $129/yr for ALL grades, some parents will assume the content is thin or unreliable. The going rate for "serious" curriculum is $300-$700/child/year.

The Van Westendorp price sensitivity for this market suggests:
- **Too cheap (suspicious)**: Under $50/yr for complete curriculum
- **Bargain / great value**: $75-$150/yr
- **Getting expensive but still worth it**: $200-$400/yr
- **Too expensive**: $500+/yr for a single grade

**You're sitting in the "bargain" zone. You could raise 50-100% and still be in the "great value" to "worth it" range.**

## VI. THE OPPORTUNITY: THREE PRICE SCENARIOS MODELED

### Scenario A: Stay at $129/yr (Current)
- **Revenue per customer**: $129/yr (annual) or $228/yr (monthly at $19)
- **Positioning**: "Cheapest complete curriculum on the market"
- **Pros**: Maximum conversion rate, easiest sell, beats everything on price
- **Cons**: Leaves money on the table, no room for upsells, may signal low quality
- **Best for**: Land grab strategy, rapid user growth, building reviews/social proof

### Scenario B: Raise to $179-$199/yr (Moderate Increase)
- **Revenue per customer**: $179-$199/yr
- **Positioning**: "Affordable premium — still 60-85% cheaper than Time4Learning"
- **Monthly anchor**: $24.99/mo (annual saves ~40%)
- **Pros**: 39-54% more revenue per customer, still dramatically cheaper, perceived as higher quality
- **Cons**: Some price-sensitive families lost, need better conversion copy
- **Best for**: MAXIMIZING PROFIT. This is the sweet spot.
- **Math**: If you have 2,400 families and convert 60% to paid at $179 = $257,760/yr revenue vs $186,480 at $129. That's $71,280 more for the same customers.

### Scenario C: Add a Premium Tier at $249-$299/yr (Tiered Strategy)
- **Revenue per customer**: $129 (basic) or $249-$299 (premium)
- **Premium features**: Progress tracking, printable worksheets, report cards, certificates, priority support, state compliance docs
- **Positioning**: "Curicaa is the affordable option. Curicaa Pro is the complete solution."
- **Pros**: Captures ESA families, higher LTV, upsell path from free/basic
- **Cons**: More features to build, splits development focus
- **Best for**: Long-term revenue maximization with a product ladder

## VII. THE RECOMMENDED STRATEGY (My Best Advice)

### Phase 1: NOW — Finish the Content, Then Raise Price

**Critical blocker**: Only 8 of 32 subject pages are deeply written. DO NOT raise prices until the content is complete. Parents who pay $129 for incomplete content will churn and leave bad reviews.

**Steps:**
1. Finish deep-writing the remaining 24 subject pages (Ages 8, 9, 10, 11-12, 15-16, 17-18)
2. Keep the "40% off — $129/yr" banner as-is while you build
3. Build the features parents say they'd pay MORE for (see below)

### Phase 2: LAUNCH — The Optimized Pricing Structure

**Recommended 3-tier pricing:**

| Tier | Monthly | Annual | What |
|------|---------|--------|------|
| **Free** | $0 | $0 | First 2 weeks of every subject + 1 full sample week per grade |
| **Curicaa** | $14.99/mo | **$149/yr** | All grades, all subjects, all children, parent guides |
| **Curicaa Pro** | $29.99/mo | **$249/yr** | Everything in Curicaa + progress tracking, printable worksheets, report cards, certificates, state compliance docs |

**Why this works:**

1. **$149/yr is a 16% increase** from $129 but still 40-70% cheaper than every paid competitor
2. **$249/yr captures ESA families** who have $5,000-$10,000 to spend and want "the full solution"
3. **The Pro tier adds features parents literally say they'd pay for**: progress tracking, printables, state compliance
4. **The free tier is slightly tightened** (2 weeks instead of 1 month) to create more upgrade friction
5. **Monthly at $14.99 makes annual look like a steal** ($180/yr vs $149/yr = 17% savings)
6. **The $249 Pro tier is STILL cheaper than Time4Learning for ONE child**

### Phase 3: OPTIMIZE — Seasonal & Psychological Tactics

**Seasonal pricing:**
- **Aug-Sep (back-to-school)**: Full price, no discounts. Peak demand.
- **Oct-Nov**: "Early bird" annual pricing at $129 for Black Friday/Cyber Monday
- **Jan (semester 2)**: Mid-year enrollment push, "Start the second semester right"
- **Mar-May**: Renewal push with loyalty pricing for existing customers

**Price anchoring improvements:**
- Keep the "$500-$2,000 traditional curriculum" anchor (it works)
- ADD: "Time4Learning charges $479/yr for ONE child. Curicaa is $149/yr for ALL your children."
- ADD: "That's $0.41/day — less than a cup of coffee."
- ADD: "IXL charges $159/yr for practice worksheets alone."

**Psychological tactics:**
- Change "$149/year" to "$149/year for the whole family" (emphasize family aspect)
- Show per-child cost for multi-child families: "3 kids? That's $49.67/child/year"
- Add an "undo" guarantee: "30-day money-back. No questions asked."
- Show the competitor comparison table MORE prominently (it's buried right now)

## VIII. FEATURES THAT DRIVE WILLINGNESS TO PAY (Build These Next)

Based on parent research, these features would justify higher pricing AND increase conversion:

### Must-Have (Justify the $149 price point)
1. **Complete all 32 subject pages** (currently only 8 done) — THIS IS CRITICAL
2. **Printable worksheets** per week (parents love physical materials)
3. **Progress tracking** (checkboxes, "weeks completed" counter)

### Should-Have (Justify the $249 Pro tier)
4. **Report cards / progress reports** (generate a PDF summary per term)
5. **State compliance documentation** (lesson plans formatted for state submission)
6. **Certificates of completion** per subject/grade
7. **Attendance tracker** (required in many states)

### Nice-to-Have (Future upsells)
8. **Community / parent forum** (parents crave connection and advice)
9. **Live Q&A sessions** (monthly "ask a teacher" — premium feature)
10. **Accreditation pathway** (partner with an umbrella school — this is the holy grail)
11. **High school transcripts** (GPA calculator, credit tracking)
