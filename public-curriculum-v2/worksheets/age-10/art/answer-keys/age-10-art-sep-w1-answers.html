ANSWER KEY — The Power of Line

Age 10 Art · September Week 1

Answers
Part 1: Line Type Matching
1.Cross-hatching→Overlapping sets of parallel lines in different directions
2.Stippling→Creating value using only small dots
3.Contour→A single continuous line without lifting the pen
4.Implied line→A line that is suggested but not actually drawn
5.Hatching→A series of parallel lines used to create shading
Part 2: Expressing Emotion with Line (Guide)

Answers will vary. Look for intentional line choices:

  • Anger: Thick, sharp, jagged, heavy-pressure lines
  • Peace: Thin, smooth, flowing, wavy, light-pressure lines
  • Chaos: Lines going in many directions, varied weight, crossing erratically
  • Joy: Bouncy, curved, upward-sweeping, energetic lines
  • Sadness: Drooping, thin, downward-curving, broken lines
  • Energy: Bold, fast, dynamic, radiating or spiraling lines
Parts 3-4: Zentangle & Line Weight

No single correct answer. Assess based on:

  • Used at least 5 distinct line types in the zentangle grid
  • Demonstrated visible variation in line weight (thin to thick)
  • Patterns show consistent repetition
Bonus Challenge: Op Art

Look for: lines that are closer together in some areas and farther apart in others to create the illusion of a curved surface. The spacing change is what creates the 3D effect.
