ANSWER KEY — Value Scales & Shading

Age 10 Art · September Week 3

Answers
Part 1: 7-Step Value Scale

Each box should be progressively darker: white, light gray, medium-light gray, medium gray, medium-dark gray, dark gray, black.

The transitions between each step should be even and smooth.

Part 2: Shading a Sphere — Labels
1. Highlight = The brightest area, closest to the light source
2. Mid-tone = The area of medium value, between highlight and shadow
3. Core shadow = The darkest part on the sphere itself, opposite the light
4. Cast shadow = The dark shape on the surface beneath/behind the sphere
5. Reflected light = A faint glow on the dark side, bounced back from the surface
Part 3: Cross-Hatching (Guide)

Light: 1 layer of widely-spaced parallel lines.

Medium: 2 layers of lines crossing at an angle.

Dark: 3+ layers crossing in multiple directions.

Very Dark: 4+ dense layers with tight spacing.

Part 4: Light Source Identification
1. Left side bright, right side dark. Light is from the: Left
2. Shadow falls to the right. Light is from the: Left
3. Top of box is brightest. Light is from: Above (or above and slightly in front)
