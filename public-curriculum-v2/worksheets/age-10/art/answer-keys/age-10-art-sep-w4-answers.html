ANSWER KEY — From Shape to Form

Age 10 Art · September Week 4

Answers
Part 1: Transform 2D to 3D

Each 3D form should show:

  • Circle → Sphere: Shaded with highlight, mid-tone, core shadow, cast shadow, and reflected light. Should look round, not flat.
  • Square → Cube: Drawn in perspective (showing 3 visible faces). Each face has a different value (top=lightest, side=medium, front=darkest).
  • Triangle → Cone: Tapered form with shading that is lighter on the side facing the light and darker on the opposite side. Elliptical base.
  • Rectangle → Cylinder: Elliptical top and bottom. Shading transitions smoothly from light to dark across the curved surface.
Part 2: Still Life (Rubric)

Assess based on:

  • Proportion: Objects relate to each other in realistic size
  • Overlapping: Closer objects overlap farther ones
  • Shading: Consistent light source, visible highlights and shadows
  • Form: Objects appear 3D, not flat
Part 3: Overlapping Spheres (Guide)

Should show 5 overlapping circles shaded to look like 3D spheres. Front spheres should be fully visible, back spheres partially hidden. Each sphere has its own highlight and shadow consistent with a single light source.

Part 4: Self-Assessment

All 5 checkboxes should be checked if the student has demonstrated mastery:

  • Light source established before starting shading
  • 5+ distinct shades visible in the work
  • 3D forms show highlight, mid-tone, and shadow
  • Overlapping used to create depth
  • Cast shadows fall in the correct direction (opposite the light)
