Part 2: Draw Shapes (Guide)

Geometric shapes: Should have straight edges, equal angles, and regular proportions. Examples: squares, pentagons, rectangles, octagons.

Organic shapes: Should be irregular, curvy, and free-form. Examples: amoebas, blobs, leaf shapes, puddles.

Part 3: Shape City (Guide)

Look for:

  • Left side uses rectangles, squares, triangles for buildings
  • Right side uses flowing, irregular shapes for landscape elements (trees, hills, water)
  • Some shapes overlap to create depth
Bonus Challenge: Tessellation

A correct tessellation uses a single repeated organic shape that fits together with no gaps and no overlaps. M.C. Escher is a famous example. The shape should interlock like puzzle pieces.
