🎨 Learning Goal

Combine shape and value to transform flat 2D shapes into 3D forms using shading and perspective.

🔸 Part 1: Transform 2D to 3D

In each pair, the left box shows a flat shape. In the right box, draw the 3D form it becomes, with shading.

🏗️ Part 2: Still Life Planning

Arrange 3 simple objects. Draw them in the box below with accurate proportions and shading to make them look 3D.

🔶 Part 3: Overlapping Forms

Draw 5 overlapping circles in the box, then use shading to turn them into a cluster of 3D spheres. Objects in front should overlap and shade those behind.

✅ Part 4: Self-Assessment

Check each box when you can honestly say you achieved it:

  • I established a clear light source before shading
  • My value scale shows at least 5 distinct shades
  • My 3D forms show highlight, mid-tone, and shadow
  • I used overlapping to create depth in my still life
  • My cast shadows fall in the correct direction
Tip: Form is created when you add VALUE (light and dark) to a SHAPE. Without shading, a circle is just a circle. With shading, it becomes a sphere.
⭐ Bonus Challenge

Draw a crumpled piece of paper from observation. Focus on capturing the complex shadows and highlights created by the folds.
