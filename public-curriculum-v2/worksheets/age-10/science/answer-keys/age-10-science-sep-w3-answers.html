❓ Part 3: Short Answer — Answers
1. Which color bean survived the most? Why?
Green beans survived the most because they blended in with the green background (camouflage), making them harder to spot and pick up.
2. Which color bean was found the most easily? Why?
White beans (or red beans) were found most easily because they stood out against the green background, making them easy to see and collect.
3. How does camouflage help an organism survive in the wild?
Camouflage helps organisms blend into their surroundings, making it harder for predators to find them. This increases their chance of surviving and reproducing.
4. If the background were white (snow), which bean color would survive best?
White beans would survive best because they would blend in with the white snowy background.
5. What does this simulation tell us about how populations change over time?
Over time, organisms with traits that help them survive (like camouflage) become more common in the population, while poorly adapted organisms become less common.
📖 Part 4: Survival of the Fittest — Answers
6. Explain "survival of the fittest" in your own words.
The organisms best adapted to their environment are more likely to survive long enough to reproduce and pass on their traits to the next generation.
7. Give one real-world example.
Accept any reasonable example: peppered moths (dark moths survived on dark trees), polar bears (white fur in snow), chameleons, arctic hares, stick insects, etc.
8. What would happen if the environment changed suddenly?
Organisms that were previously well-adapted might struggle to survive. Traits that were once helpful might become harmful. The population would need to adapt, migrate, or face decline.
