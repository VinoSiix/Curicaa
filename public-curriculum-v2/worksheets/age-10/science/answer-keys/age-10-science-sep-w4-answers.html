🧠 Part 4: Mnemonic and Short Answer — Answers
1. Create your own mnemonic:
Accept any mnemonic where each word starts with D, K, P, C, O, F, G, S. Example: "Daring Kangaroos Play Chess On Fridays, Generally Speaking."
2. Why do scientists classify living things?
To organize and communicate about the enormous diversity of life. Classification helps scientists identify organisms, understand relationships between species, and study evolutionary history.
3. What is the difference between genus and species in a scientific name?
The genus is a broader group that includes closely related species. The species is the most specific level — it identifies one particular type of organism. Together they form a unique two-part name (binomial nomenclature).
4. Write the scientific name for humans:
Homo sapiens (genus capitalized, species lowercase, both italicized or underlined)
