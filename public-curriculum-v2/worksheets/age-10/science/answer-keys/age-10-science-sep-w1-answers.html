🔬 Part 2: Label the DNA Diagram — Answers
Sugar-phosphate backbone: The vertical rails on the left and right sides of the ladder (the "——" lines)
Base pair: The horizontal rungs connecting the two strands (A-T and C-G pairs)
Nucleotide: One full unit = one sugar + one phosphate + one base (e.g., the backbone segment + its base on one side)

❓ Part 4: Short Answer — Answers
1. What are the three parts of a nucleotide?
Sugar (deoxyribose), phosphate group, and a nitrogenous base.
2. Why is DNA replication important before a cell divides?
Each new cell needs a complete copy of the DNA instructions. Without replication, one cell would have no genetic information.
3. If a DNA strand has 20% adenine, what percentage of thymine does it have?
20% thymine. Adenine always pairs with thymine in equal amounts (Chargaff's rule). The remaining 60% would be 30% cytosine and 30% guanine.
4. Describe the "double helix" shape.
Two strands twisted around each other like a spiral staircase or twisted ladder.
5. What would happen if the wrong base was paired during replication?
It would create a mutation — a change in the DNA sequence. This could change the instructions and affect how the cell works.
⭐ Bonus Challenge Answer: There are 70 cytosine-guanine pairs. Total = 100 pairs. 30 are A-T pairs. 100 − 30 = 70 C-G pairs.
