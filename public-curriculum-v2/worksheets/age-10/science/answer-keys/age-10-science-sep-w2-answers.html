❓ Part 4: Vocabulary Fill-In — Answers
1. A gene is a segment of DNA that codes for a specific trait.
2. An organism with two identical alleles (e.g., TT or tt) is called homozygous.
3. An organism with two different alleles (e.g., Tt) is called heterozygous.
4. The phenotype is the physical appearance of a trait.
5. The genotype is the genetic makeup (allele combination).
6. A dominant allele masks the effect of a recessive allele.
