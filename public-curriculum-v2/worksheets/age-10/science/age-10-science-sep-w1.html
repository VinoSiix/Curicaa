🎯 Learning Goal

Understand the structure of DNA, including base pairing rules (A-T, C-G), and explain how DNA replicates before cell division.

🧬 Part 1: Base Pairing Rules

Remember the base pairing rules: Adenine (A) pairs with Thymine (T), and Cytosine (C) pairs with Guanine (G). Complete the complementary strand for each DNA sequence:

#Original Strand (5' → 3')Complementary Strand (3' → 5')
1A — T — C — G — G — A 
2T — C — A — G — T — T 
3G — G — A — T — C — C 
4A — A — C — G — T — A 
5C — T — G — A — C — G 
6T — A — G — C — A — T

Match each term to its definition by writing the correct letter in the box:

3Double helix C. Two complementary bases held together by hydrogen bonds
4Replication D. A base found in DNA that pairs with guanine

❓ Part 4: Short Answer
1. What are the three parts of a nucleotide?  
2. Why is DNA replication important before a cell divides?  
3. If a DNA strand has 20% adenine, what percentage of thymine does it have? Explain.  
4. Describe in your own words what the "double helix" shape looks like.  
5. What would happen if the wrong base was paired during replication?  
⭐ Bonus Challenge: A DNA molecule has 100 base pairs. If 30 of them are adenine-thymine pairs, how many cytosine-guanine pairs are there? Explain how you worked it out.
💡 Easier: Focus only on the four base letters (A, T, C, G) and their pairing rules before attempting the labeling activity.
💡 Harder: Research what mRNA is and explain how the bases differ in RNA (uracil replaces thymine).
