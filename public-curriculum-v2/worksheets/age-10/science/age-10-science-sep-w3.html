🎯 Learning Goal

Understand how natural selection works by simulating how camouflage affects survival across generations. Explain "survival of the fittest" in your own words.

🔬 Part 1: Simulation — Bean Hunt

Instructions: Spread 20 red, 20 green, and 20 white beans on green grass (or a green cloth). Have a partner collect as many beans as they can find in 30 seconds. Record the survivors. Repeat for 3 generations (replenish survivors to 20 each color each round).

❓ Part 3: Short Answer — Adaptation and Camouflage
1. Which color bean survived the most? Why?  
2. Which color bean was found the most easily? Why?  
3. How does camouflage help an organism survive in the wild?  
4. If the background were white (like snow) instead of green, which bean color do you think would survive best?  
5. What does this simulation tell us about how populations change over time?  
📖 Part 4: Survival of the Fittest

In biology, "fitness" does not mean the strongest or fastest. It means how well an organism survives and reproduces in its environment.
