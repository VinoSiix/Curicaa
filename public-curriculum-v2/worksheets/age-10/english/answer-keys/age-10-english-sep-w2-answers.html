ANSWER KEY
☀️ Warm-Up: Quick Character Think

Answers will vary. Accept any character from any book, film, or show. Check that:

  • Character name is specific
  • Personality word is a trait (e.g. brave, kind, sneaky) — not an appearance word
  • The action described actually shows the trait mentioned
🧍 Activity: Character Outline Organizer

Answers vary by character chosen. Check for correct categorisation:

Outside Traits should be physical: hair colour, height, clothing, eye colour, scars, etc.

Inside Traits should be personality: brave, curious, kind, stubborn, funny, shy, etc.

Common mistake: Students put personality words in the "outside" column. Redirect if needed.

🔍 Activity: The STEAL Method

Answers vary by text. For each row, check that:

  • S — Speech: A direct quote of something the character says
  • T — Thoughts: What the character thinks/feels (from narration or inner dialogue)
  • E — Effect on Others: How other characters react to or feel about this character
  • A — Actions: Something the character does (a behaviour or decision)
  • L — Looks: A description of the character's physical appearance

The summary should synthesise evidence into a personality description (2–3 sentences).

🏷️ Activity: Trait Word Bank Matching

This section has definitive answers:

1. Willing to give and share freely with others   D

3. Eager to learn and explore new things   B
4. Ready to face danger or difficulty without fear   A
5. Sure of oneself and one's abilities   F

📝 Activity: Quote Analysis

Suggested answers — accept similar trait words and reasonable explanations:

Quote 1: "I don't care if it takes all night. I'm going to finish this puzzle."

determined / persistent / stubborn — because they refuse to give up until the task is done

Quote 2: "Here, you can have my last piece of cake. You look like you need it more than me."

generous / kind / caring — because they are willing to give up something for someone else's benefit

Quote 3: "I know I can do this. I've practised a hundred times."

confident / self-assured / prepared — because they believe in their ability due to hard work and practice

⭐ Bonus Challenge:

Accept any comparison paragraph that includes: at least one similarity, at least one difference, specific examples from the text. Look for 4–5 complete sentences.
