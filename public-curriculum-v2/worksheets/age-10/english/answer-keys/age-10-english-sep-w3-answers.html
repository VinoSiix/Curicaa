ANSWER KEY
☀️ Warm-Up: Parts of a Story

Answers will vary by story chosen. Accept any well-known story. Check that:

  • Story is named specifically
  • Beginning describes the setup (characters/setting)
  • Exciting part identifies the climax or key conflict
  • Ending describes the resolution
⛰️ Activity: Plot Mountain Diagram

Answers vary by story. Check that each box contains an appropriate event for that stage:

  • Exposition: Introduces main characters and setting (e.g. "Red lives with her mother near a forest")
  • Rising Action: Events that build tension (e.g. "Red meets the wolf; the wolf races ahead")
  • Climax: The most exciting/important moment (e.g. "Red discovers the wolf in Granny's bed")
  • Falling Action: Events after the peak (e.g. "The woodcutter arrives and rescues them")
  • Resolution: How things end (e.g. "Red and Granny are safe; Red learns not to talk to strangers")
🔢 Activity: Story Event Sequencing

Correct order for "Little Red Riding Hood":

1 — Red sets off to visit her grandmother
2 — Red meets the wolf in the forest
3 — The wolf disguises himself as Granny
4 — The woodcutter saves Little Red
5 — Red and Granny celebrate together
🔗 Activity: Plot Element Matching

Correct matches:

1. Exposition = c — The introduction of characters, setting, and situation
2. Rising Action = d — Events build tension and lead toward the peak
3. Climax = a — The turning point or most intense moment of the story
4. Falling Action = b — The problems start to resolve and things calm down
5. Resolution = e — The story wraps up and loose ends are tied
📝 Activity: Story Analysis

Answers vary by story. Check for understanding of each element:

  • Exposition: Names main characters and describes setting (time and place)
  • Climax: Identifies the most tense or exciting turning point specifically
  • Resolution: Explains how the main conflict/problem was resolved

Each response should be 1–2 complete sentences with specific details from the story.

⭐ Bonus Challenge:

Accept any original story outline that follows the five-stage plot structure correctly. Check that:

  • Rising action creates a problem or tension
  • Climax is the peak moment
  • Falling action shows things calming down
  • Resolution wraps up the story
