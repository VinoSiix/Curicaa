ANSWER KEY
☀️ Warm-Up: My Reading Right Now

Answers will vary. Accept any reasonable, thoughtful response from the student.

1. Book title — student's current book

2. Pages read — any reasonable number (5–50+)

3. Enjoyment rating — 1–5 scale, any number

4. Reading time before distraction — any reasonable time (5–45+ mins)

✋ Activity: The 5-Finger Rule

Answers depend on the book the student selects. Check that the student correctly applies the rule:

  • 0–1 unknown words = too easy
  • 2–3 unknown words = just right
  • 4–5 unknown words = too hard

Finger count: varies   Verdict: should match finger count

📖 Activity: "Just Right" Book Evaluation

Answers vary by book. Check that scores are reasonable and the verdict matches:

1. Understanding score: 1–5

2. Word-reading score: 1–5

3. Interest score: 1–5

Total: 3–15   12–15 = just right, 8–11 = borderline, 3–7 = probably not the right fit

📋 Activity: Weekly Reading Log

This is a tracking exercise — answers are unique to each student's week. Look for:

  • Book title and author filled in each day
  • Page count recorded
  • Summary is 2–3 sentences that describe what happened
  • Total pages calculated correctly from individual entries
🎯 Activity: Set Your Reading Goal

Answers vary. A good goal is specific, measurable, and realistic. Look for:

  • Page count per day: reasonable (10–40+ pages)
  • Number of days: 4–5 days
  • A specific time of day (e.g. "after dinner", "before bed")
  • A specific reading spot (e.g. "my bedroom", "the sofa")
  • Explanation shows thoughtful reasoning
📅 Daily Stamina Tracker

Tracking exercise — accept any reasonable minutes recorded. Students should see their stamina increase over the week.

Target: 15–30 minutes per day by end of week. A good goal is to add 2–5 minutes each day.

⭐ Bonus Challenge:

Accept any paragraph that summarises what happened during the 30-minute session. Look for: key events in order, character names used, and complete sentences.
