Reading Comprehension
ANSWER KEY
☀️ Warm-Up: Topic vs Theme

Theme statements will vary. Accept any complete sentence that expresses a lesson or message about the topic. Suggested answers:

Topic: Courage
Theme: Being brave doesn't mean you aren't scared — it means you do the right thing even when you are.
Topic: Honesty
Theme: Telling the truth is always better than lying, even when the truth is hard to say.
Topic: Perseverance
Theme: If you keep trying and never give up, you can achieve things you never thought possible.

A theme statement should be a full sentence that expresses a universal lesson, not just a single word.

📊 Activity: Character Lesson to Theme (T-Chart)

Answers vary by story. Check that the right column correctly turns a character's experience into a universal statement:

Example using Charlotte's Web:

What the character learned: Wilbur learned that a true friend will sacrifice their own time and energy to help you.
Theme statement: True friendship means putting others before yourself.

The theme statement should generalise the lesson — it should apply beyond just this one story.

🔗 Activity: Match the Theme to the Story

Correct matches:

1. The mouse practises daily and wins   D — Believe in yourself
2. The rough dragon just wants a friend   B — Appearances can be deceiving
3. Holding a grudge only hurts himself   C — It is important to forgive others
4. Boy trains for months, finishes first   A — Hard work pays off
🔎 Activity: Find the Evidence

Answers vary by story. Check for:

  • Story title is a real book or story the student has read
  • Theme statement is a complete sentence expressing a universal lesson
  • Evidence 1 is a specific moment, quote, or paraphrase from the text
  • Evidence 2 is a different specific moment (not the same scene)
  • The explanation clearly connects the evidence to the theme
✍️ Activity: Write Your Own Theme Statement

Passage about Mia and her brother:

Topic: Helping / Kindness / Family / Selflessness

Theme (suggested):

Helping others even when it means giving up something you want is one of the most rewarding things you can do.

Evidence — accept any of these:

  • "Mia helped her younger brother with his reading homework even though she wanted to go outside and play"
  • "her brother read his first full sentence by himself"
  • "'I couldn't have done it without you'"
  • "He looked up at Mia with the biggest smile"

The student should explain how Mia's sacrifice led to a meaningful outcome for her brother.

⭐ Bonus Challenge:

Accept any two stories that share a theme. Look for:

  • Two different stories named (books, films, fairy tales, etc.)
  • A clear shared theme statement
  • Explanation of how the theme appears in each story with at least one specific example from each

Example: Harry Potter and The Lord of the Rings both share the theme that courage means standing up to evil even when you are small or afraid. Harry faces Voldemort despite being just a boy; Frodo carries the ring despite being a small hobbit.
