Warm vs Cool Color Schemes

Age 9 Art & PE · October Week 2

Age 9

Painting
🎯 Learning Goal

Understand the emotional and visual impact of warm and cool color palettes and apply them to an original painting.

☀️ Warm-Up

Sort colored cards or paint swatches into two piles -- warm (reds, oranges, yellows) and cool (blues, greens, purples) -- as fast as you can.

🎨 Activity: Warm vs Cool Color Schemes

Fold paper in half. On the left, paint a landscape using ONLY warm colors. On the right, paint the same type using ONLY cool colors. Compare how they feel different. Write one sentence under each describing the mood.

🎨 Practice: Warm vs Cool Paintings

Paint a warm-color landscape on the left and a cool-color landscape on the right. Write the mood below each.

WARM colors (reds, oranges, yellows)
COOL colors (blues, greens, purples)

Warm mood: ____________________________________

Cool mood: ____________________________________

💡 Reflection Questions

1. What was the most challenging part of this week's activity?

2. What would you do differently next time?

✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Correctly mixed all 12 color wheel colors
  • Can name which two colors mix to make any tertiary color
  • Correctly classified colors as warm or cool
  • Created two paintings with contrasting moods through color
💡 Tip: Easier: Paint abstract color fields instead of landscapes. Harder: Paint one scene using both warm and cool colors for contrast.
