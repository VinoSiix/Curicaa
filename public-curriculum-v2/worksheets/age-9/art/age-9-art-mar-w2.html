Photo Essay: Telling a Visual Story

Age 9 Art & PE · March Week 2

Age 9

Photography
🎯 Learning Goal

Plan and shoot a 5-8 photo essay that tells a sequential story without words, arranging photos in a deliberate order.

☀️ Warm-Up

5-Photo Story practice: Arrange 5 random objects in a line. Take one photo of each. Can someone else tell what's happening?

📷 Activity: Photo Essay: Telling a Visual Story

Choose a story topic. Plan 6 photos on a storyboard (grid of 6 boxes with sketch and note). Shoot all 6 photos using composition rules. Print or display in order. Add a title only -- no captions.

📷 Practice: Photo Storyboard

Plan your 6-photo essay below. Sketch and describe each shot:

My Story Topic: _____________________________________

Photo 1: ___________
Photo 2: ___________
Photo 3: ___________
Photo 4: ___________
Photo 5: ___________
Photo 6: ___________

Title for my photo essay: _____________________________________

💡 Reflection Questions

1. What was the most challenging part of this week's activity?

2. What would you do differently next time?

✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Took photos demonstrating rule of thirds, framing, and leading lines
  • Planned a 6-photo storyboard with clear sequence
  • Can identify which composition technique was used in each photo
  • Created a photo essay that tells a story without words
💡 Tip: Easier: Tell the story in 4 photos. Harder: Write a 2-sentence caption for each and create a digital slideshow.
