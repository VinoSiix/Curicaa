Strategy Games & Fair Play

Age 9 Art & PE · May Week 4

Age 9

Leadership
🎯 Learning Goal

Play team games that develop strategic thinking and practice winning and losing with grace and sportsmanship.

☀️ Warm-Up

Rock-Paper-Scissors Tournament: Best of 3. After each match, shake hands and say Good game.

🥇 Activity: Strategy Games & Fair Play

Game 1: Capture the Flag. Game 2: Steal the Bacon. Game 3: Modified Kickball. After all games, hold a Sportsmanship Circle -- each person shares one compliment for an opponent.

🤝 Practice: Sportsmanship & Strategy

After each game, reflect on strategy and sportsmanship:

GameOur StrategyDid It Work?
Capture the Flag
Steal the Bacon
Kickball

Compliment I gave an opponent:

Why is sportsmanship more important than winning?

💡 Reflection Questions

1. What was the most challenging part of this week's activity?

2. What would you do differently next time?

✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Took on a leadership role in at least one challenge
  • Delegated tasks clearly
  • Demonstrated fair play and encouraged teammates
  • Can articulate why sportsmanship matters more than winning
💡 Tip: Easier: Play 2 games with smaller teams. Harder: Student designs rules for a new strategy game and teaches it.
