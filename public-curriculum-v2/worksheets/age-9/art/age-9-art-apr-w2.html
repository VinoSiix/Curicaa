🎯 Learning Goal

Understand visual hierarchy and create a poster or logo using C.A.R.P. design principles -- Contrast, Alignment, Repetition, Proximity.

☀️ Warm-Up

Bad Design Hunt: Find 3 examples of poorly designed signs, ads, or packaging. Identify what makes them hard to read.

📷 Activity: Graphic Design: Posters & Logos

Learn C.A.R.P.: Contrast, Alignment, Repetition, Proximity. Design a poster for a fictional event. Include title, date/time/location, illustration, 2-3 colors. Sketch 3 rough layouts first, then create final.

🎨 Practice: C.A.R.P. Design Principles

Apply each design principle to your poster:

PrincipleWhat It MeansHow I Applied It
ContrastMake important things stand out
AlignmentEverything lines up neatly
RepetitionRepeat colors, fonts, styles
ProximityGroup related items together

✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Successfully used layers, brushes, and color picker
  • Can explain what a "layer" is and why it's useful
  • Created a poster using all 4 C.A.R.P. principles
  • Used no more than 3 colors in the design
💡 Tip: Easier: Design just a logo instead of a full poster. Harder: Design a two-page flyer with front and back.
