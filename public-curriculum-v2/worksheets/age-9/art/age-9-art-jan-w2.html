Cubism & Picasso

Age 9 Art & PE · January Week 2

Age 9

Art History
🎯 Learning Goal

Explore Pablo Picasso's Cubist style by creating an abstract portrait that shows multiple perspectives simultaneously.

☀️ Warm-Up

Draw a simple face from the front, then from the side. Cut both out and overlap them slightly.

🎨 Activity: Cubism & Picasso

Create a Cubist portrait: Draw an oval face. Draw a side-view eye and nose on one half, front-view eye on other half. Divide background into angular shapes. Color each shape with bold colors. Add patterns to some shapes.

🖌️ Practice: Create a Cubist Portrait

Draw a face that combines multiple perspectives. Use geometric shapes and bold colors!

My Cubist Portrait

Perspectives I combined:

  • A side-view eye
  • A front-view eye
  • A side-view nose
  • Geometric background shapes
  • Bold, contrasting colors
  • Patterns (stripes or dots)

✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Used short, visible brushstrokes like Monet
  • Created a portrait combining multiple perspectives
  • Can explain one way their work is "Impressionist" or "Cubist"
  • Used geometric shapes and bold colors in Cubist portrait
💡 Tip: Easier: Use a pre-drawn head shape. Harder: Create a full Cubist still life using only geometric shapes.
