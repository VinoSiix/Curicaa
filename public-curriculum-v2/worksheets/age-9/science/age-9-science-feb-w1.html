Age 9 Science · February Week 1

Plant Structures & Functions
🎯 Learning Goal

Dissect a flower and identify its major structures, explaining the function of roots, stems, leaves, and flowers.

☀️ Warm-Up

Go outside and carefully pull up a small weed with roots intact. Lay it on paper and label the parts you can see: roots, stem, leaves, and any flower buds.

🔬 Activity: Flower Dissection

Dissect a large flower and tape each part to paper:

PartWhat It Looks LikeIts Function
Sepals Protects the bud
Petals Attracts pollinators
Stamen (anther + filament) Makes pollen
Pistil (stigma + style + ovary) Receives pollen, makes seeds

✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Correctly label all major flower parts after dissection
  • State the function of roots, stems, leaves, and flowers
  • Identify male and female flower parts
  • Connect plant parts to vegetables we eat
💡 Easier: Focus on the four basic parts (root, stem, leaf, flower) without detailed dissection.
💡 Harder: Identify male vs. female parts and discuss why some flowers have only one.
