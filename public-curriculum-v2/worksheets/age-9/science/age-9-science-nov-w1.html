Wave Properties: Amplitude, Wavelength, Frequency

Age 9 Science · November Week 1

Age 9

Waves & Information Transfer
🎯 Learning Goal

Describe waves using the terms amplitude (height), wavelength (distance between peaks), and frequency (how often waves pass a point) and demonstrate each with a slinky.

☀️ Warm-Up

Drop a pebble into a large bowl of water and watch the ripples. Count how many ripples reach the edge in 5 seconds — that's related to frequency.

🔬 Activity: Slinky Wave Investigation

Stretch a slinky between two people. Practice transverse (side-to-side) and longitudinal (push-pull) waves:

✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Point out amplitude and wavelength on a wave drawing
  • Explain that higher amplitude carries more energy
  • Demonstrate transverse and longitudinal waves
  • Define frequency in their own words
💡 Easier: Focus on amplitude only (big waves vs. small waves).
💡 Harder: Introduce frequency × wavelength = wave speed. All sound waves travel at the same speed in air.
