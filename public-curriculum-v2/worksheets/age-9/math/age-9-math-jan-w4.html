Multi-Step Word Problems with All Four Operations

Age 9 Math · January Week 4

Age 9

Multi-Digit Multiplication & Division
🎯 Learning Goal

Solve multi-step word problems using all four operations, including interpreting remainders.

☀️ Warm-Up

Mental math: 8 boxes of 12 pencils. Sell 45. How many left?

✏️ Practice: Two-Step Problems

Show each step!

1. School has 186 students. Buses hold 45. How many buses needed? Total cost at $125/bus?  
2. Store: 8 boxes of 24 pencils. Sell 67. How many left?  
3. Garden: 15 rows of 18 plants. 42 die. How many survive?  
📝 Practice: Three-Step Problems

Read twice. Identify steps. Show all work.

4. Movie tickets: $12/adult, $8/kid. 2 adults + 3 kids go. Popcorn $15. Total? Pay $60, change?  
5. Bake sale: 12 dozen cookies (12 per dozen). Sell 89. Box remaining into 7 equal boxes. How many per box?  
6. Library: 245 books/shelf, 6 shelves. Lend out 347. How many remain?  
7. Savings: $5/week for 24 weeks. Spend $67 on a bike. How much saved?  
8. Farmer: 348 apples. Packs into bags of 8. Sells 32 bags at $3 each. Revenue?  
9. Class reads 32 pages/day for 5 days. Book is 200 pages. Do they finish? How many pages left or extra?  
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Break multi-step problems into individual operations
  • Choose correct operation for each step
  • Interpret remainders in context
  • Check answers for reasonableness
💡 Easier: Two-step problems with addition and multiplication only.
💡 Harder: Three-step problems involving all four operations.
