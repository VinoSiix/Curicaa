Multi-Step Real-World Problem Solving

Age 9 Math · May Week 1

Age 9

Advanced Problem Solving & Review
🎯 Learning Goal

Solve complex multi-step word problems involving all four operations, fractions, and decimals using a systematic 5-step approach.

☀️ Warm-Up

Family of 4 at a restaurant. Each meal $12.50, 2 desserts at $6.75 each, $10 tip. Total bill?

🧮 The 5-Step Framework

Use this for every problem:

  1. Understand — Read twice, underline key info
  2. Plan — Choose a strategy
  3. Solve — Show all work, one step at a time
  4. Check — Does the answer make sense?
  5. Communicate — Write a complete sentence answer
✏️ Practice: Apply the Framework

Show all 5 steps for each problem.

1. 352 students go on a trip. Each bus holds 48. How many buses? Cost: $275/bus. Budget $2,000. Enough?  
2. BBQ for 24 people. Each eats 3/4 lb meat. Meat costs $5.50/lb. Total cost?  
3. A store has 15 boxes of 24 pencils. They sell 187. How many boxes remain (whole boxes)?  
📝 Practice: More Complex Problems
4. You buy 3 items: $14.99, $8.50, $6.75. Pay with $50. Change?  
5. A recipe needs 2 1/3 cups flour. You make 3 batches. Total flour?  
6. Room: 14 ft × 12 ft. Carpet $8/sq ft. Budget $1,500. Enough?  
7. A field is 80 m long. You run 6 laps. How many km?  
8. Library has 2,450 books. 357 fiction, 489 non-fiction, rest reference. Reference books?  
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Apply the 5-step problem-solving framework
  • Solve three-step problems independently
  • Show organized work with labels
  • Check answers for reasonableness
💡 Easier: Two-step problems with whole numbers only.
💡 Harder: Problems combining all skills (fractions, decimals, geometry).
