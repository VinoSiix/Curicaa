Math Autobiography & Reflection

Age 9 Math · June Week 1

Age 9

Portfolio & Celebration
🎯 Learning Goal

Reflect on mathematical growth throughout Grade 4 by writing a math autobiography with specific examples.

☀️ Warm-Up

Look at your first math assignment from September. What did it cover? How does it feel now?

🧮 Activity: Write Your Math Autobiography

Write 3-5 sentences for each section:

  1. Who I Am as a Mathematician — Describe your math identity.
  2. My Biggest Challenge — Be specific. What was hard? What helped?
  3. My Favorite Topic — Why? Give a specific example.
  4. A Skill I Want to Keep Working On — Set a goal.
  5. How I Use Math in Real Life — Three specific examples.
✏️ Practice: Year in Review

Answer each question in 2-3 sentences:

1. What math topic was hardest for you this year? What made it click?
2. What topic was easiest? Why?
3. Name a time you used math outside of school this year.
4. If you could give advice to next year's students, what would you say?
5. What is one math goal for next year?
6. Draw a "math timeline" with one milestone per month.
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Write a thoughtful math autobiography
  • Identify specific strengths and challenges
  • Use correct math vocabulary in reflections
  • Set a goal for next year
💡 Easier: Use sentence starters for each paragraph.
💡 Harder: Include analysis of a specific mistake and what was learned.
