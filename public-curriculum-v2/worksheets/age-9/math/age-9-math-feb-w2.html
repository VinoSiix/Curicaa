Classifying Two-Dimensional Figures

Age 9 Math · February Week 2

Age 9

Geometry
🎯 Learning Goal

Classify two-dimensional figures based on parallel/perpendicular lines and angle types. Recognize right triangles.

☀️ Warm-Up

Draw a square, rectangle, and rhombus. What do they share? How are they different?

✏️ Practice: Quadrilateral Properties

Fill in the chart (YES or NO):

1. Square: 4 right angles? ___ 4 equal sides? ___ Opposite sides parallel? ___
2. Rectangle: 4 right angles? ___ 4 equal sides? ___ Opposite sides parallel? ___
3. Rhombus: 4 right angles? ___ 4 equal sides? ___ Opposite sides parallel? ___
4. Trapezoid: One pair parallel? ___ 4 equal sides? ___ 4 right angles? ___
🔢 Practice: Classify Triangles

By angles:

5. Right triangle: has one ___° angle.
6. Acute triangle: ALL angles are ___ than 90°.
7. Obtuse triangle: has ONE angle ___ than 90°.
📝 Practice: Name That Shape
8. 4 sides, no right angles, only one pair parallel. It is a ___________.
9. 4 equal sides, 4 right angles. It is a ___________.
10. A triangle with angles 90°, 45°, 45° is a _________ triangle.
11. A shape with 4 sides, opposite sides parallel, but not all sides equal and not all right angles is a ___________.
12. True or False: A square is a rectangle. Explain.  
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Classify quadrilaterals by properties
  • Identify right, acute, and obtuse triangles
  • Explain why a square is a special rectangle
  • Sort shapes into correct categories
💡 Easier: Sort by number of sides first (triangles, quadrilaterals, pentagons).
💡 Harder: Create a Venn diagram showing parallelogram relationships.
