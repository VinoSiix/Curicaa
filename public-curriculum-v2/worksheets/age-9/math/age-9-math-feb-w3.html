Lines of Symmetry

Age 9 Math · February Week 3

Age 9

Geometry
🎯 Learning Goal

Recognize a line of symmetry for a two-dimensional figure. Identify line-symmetric figures and draw lines of symmetry.

☀️ Warm-Up

Fold paper in half. Cut a heart shape from the fold. Unfold. Why do both sides match?

✏️ Practice: Count Lines of Symmetry

How many lines of symmetry does each have?

1. Square:   lines
2. Rectangle:   lines

4. Circle:   lines (hint: infinitely many!)
5. Scalene triangle:   lines

📝 Practice: Symmetry Drawing & Identification
7. Draw the mirror image to complete: half a butterfly along a vertical line.
8. Is the letter A symmetric?   What about B?  
9. Draw all lines of symmetry on a square (there should be 4).
10. Name 3 things in nature that have symmetry: ___, ___, ___
11. Draw a shape with exactly one line of symmetry.
12. Can a rectangle have diagonal lines of symmetry?   Explain.
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Identify all lines of symmetry in shapes
  • Draw a complete figure given half
  • Count symmetry lines for common shapes
  • Find symmetry in nature and everyday objects
💡 Easier: Shapes with obvious single symmetry lines using actual folding.
💡 Harder: Determine planes of symmetry in 3D objects.
