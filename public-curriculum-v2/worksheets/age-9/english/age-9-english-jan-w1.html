Four Sentence Types

January Week 1

Age 9

Grammar & Mechanics
🎯 Learning Goal

Identify and write the four sentence types: declarative (statement), interrogative (question), imperative (command), exclamatory (strong emotion) with correct end punctuation.

☀️ Warm-Up: Sentence Type Sort

Read each sentence and hold up the correct card: D (Declarative), I (Interrogative), IM (Imperative), or E (Exclamatory).

📝 Activity: Sentence Types Reference

Create a reference poster with four quadrants:

  • Declarative: Tells something, ends with a period. ("The dog chased the ball.")
  • Interrogative: Asks something, ends with a question mark. ("Where did the ball go?")
  • Imperative: Gives a command, ends with a period. ("Go get the ball.")
  • Exclamatory: Shows strong emotion, ends with an exclamation mark. ("That was the best catch ever!")

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Write 4 sentences (one of each type) about the same topic
  • Correctly identify sentence types in a paragraph with 90% accuracy
  • Use correct end punctuation for each sentence type
  • Explain why authors use different sentence types
💡 Easier: Focus on declarative and interrogative first, then add the others.
💡 Harder: Identify sentences that could be two types depending on context and discuss why.
