Similes & Metaphors

December Week 2

Age 9

Figurative Language
🎯 Learning Goal

Identify and write similes (comparisons using "like" or "as") and metaphors (direct comparisons saying one thing IS another) in poetry and prose.

☀️ Warm-Up: Quick-Fire Similes

Complete each phrase with an ORIGINAL comparison (not the usual ones!):

  1. "As cold as ___"
  2. "As fast as ___"
  3. "As quiet as ___"
  4. "As bright as ___"
📝 Activity: Simile vs. Metaphor

A simile uses "like" or "as" to compare: "Her eyes sparkled like stars."

A metaphor says something IS something else: "Her eyes were sparkling stars."

Read 3 poems with figurative language. Highlight similes in one color and metaphors in another. Then write an "I Am" poem with 3 similes and 3 metaphors.

  6. The old man's voice was gravel crunching underfoot.   _____
  7. She was as brave as a lion facing danger.   _____
  8. The classroom was a zoo when the teacher stepped out.   _____

Part B: Write 3 original similes.

  1. As _____ as _____

Part C: Write 3 original metaphors.

  1. _____ is _____

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Correctly identify similes and metaphors in a poem
  • Write 3 original similes with vivid comparisons
  • Write 3 original metaphors with vivid imagery
  • Convert between similes and metaphors
💡 Easier: Focus on similes only, which are easier to construct.
💡 Harder: Write an extended metaphor poem where the entire poem develops one metaphor across multiple lines.
