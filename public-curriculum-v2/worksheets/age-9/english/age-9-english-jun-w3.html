Age 9

Portfolio & Reflection
🎯 Learning Goal

Present a selected writing piece to an audience with confidence, using public speaking skills practiced throughout the year.

☀️ Warm-Up: The 3 P's

Practice the 3 P's: Posture (stand tall), Projection (speak so the back row can hear), Pace (not too fast, not too slow). Read a paragraph aloud 3 times, focusing on one P each time.

📝 Activity: Author's Chair Prep

Select your absolute best piece from the year. Prepare:

  1. Type or neatly handwrite the final version on special paper
  2. Create a visual aid (poster, prop, or illustration)
  3. Practice reading aloud 5 times: twice alone, twice for parent, once as dress rehearsal
  4. On presentation day: introduce yourself, read with expression, make eye contact, thank the audience, invite questions

Part B: Write your introduction and closing.

Introduction: "Hi, my name is ___ and I'm going to read ___ because ___"

 

Closing: "Thank you for listening. I chose this piece because ___"

 

Part C: Q&A Preparation — Write answers to possible audience questions.

  1. Why did you choose this topic?  
  2. What was the hardest part to write?  
  3. How long did it take you?  
  4. What's your favorite line in the piece?  
  5. What would you change if you wrote it again?

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Present writing with clear voice, steady pace, and eye contact
  • Read with expression and appropriate pauses
  • Answer audience questions thoughtfully
  • Demonstrate pride in your work
💡 Easier: Read from the paper with focus on volume and pace only.
💡 Harder: Present without reading, using only note cards, and include an interactive element.
