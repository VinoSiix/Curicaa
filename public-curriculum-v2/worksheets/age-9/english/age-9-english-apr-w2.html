Context Clues Strategies

April Week 2

Age 9

Vocabulary & Word Study
🎯 Learning Goal

Use four types of context clues (definition, synonym, antonym, example) to determine the meaning of unknown words in grade-level text.

☀️ Warm-Up: Nonsense Word

"The flibbertigibbet was so delicious that I ate three helpings." What does "flibbertigibbet" mean? How do you know? The words around it told you — that's a context clue!

📝 Activity: Four Types of Context Clues
  1. Definition: Meaning is stated in the text ("The arid desert, a dry and sandy place, stretched for miles.")
  2. Synonym: A similar word nearby ("The meek, or timid, kitten hid.")
  3. Antonym: An opposite word nearby ("Unlike her boisterous brother, Maria was quiet.")
  4. Example: Examples hint at meaning ("Marine animals, such as whales, dolphins, and seals...")

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Correctly identify the context clue type in 6 out of 8 sentences
  • Write reasonable definitions for at least 6 unfamiliar words using context
  • Name all 4 types of context clues
  • Use context clues during independent reading
💡 Easier: Focus on definition and example clues, which are the most direct.
💡 Harder: Read a passage where no single clue type is obvious and combine multiple clues to determine meaning.
