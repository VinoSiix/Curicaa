Exploring Poetry Forms

December Week 1

Age 9

Poetry
🎯 Learning Goal

Identify and write four types of poems: acrostic, haiku, cinquain, and free verse.

☀️ Warm-Up: Poetry Reading

Read 4 short poems from a children's poetry book (try Where the Sidewalk Ends by Shel Silverstein). Discuss: How are these poems different from each other?

📝 Activity: Poetry Workshop

Learn one poetry form each day:

  1. Acrostic: First letter of each line spells a word vertically (e.g., WINTER — White snow, I see my breath...)
  2. Haiku: 3 lines, 5-7-5 syllable pattern
  3. Cinquain: 5 lines: 1 noun, 2 adjectives, 3 -ing verbs, 4-word phrase, 1 synonym
  4. Free Verse: No rules — express feelings through line breaks and stanzas

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Write 4 poems (one of each type)
  • Follow the correct structure for each form
  • Use creative word choices in your poems
  • Count syllables correctly for haiku
💡 Easier: Focus on acrostic and haiku only, which have the most concrete rules.
💡 Harder: Write a narrative poem that tells a story with characters, a problem, and a resolution across at least 3 stanzas.
