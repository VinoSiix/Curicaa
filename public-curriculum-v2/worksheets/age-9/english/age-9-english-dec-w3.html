Personification, Onomatopoeia & Alliteration

December Week 3

Age 9

Figurative Language
🎯 Learning Goal

Use personification (giving human qualities to non-human things), onomatopoeia (sound words), and alliteration (repeated beginning sounds) in writing.

☀️ Warm-Up: Sound Words

Name as many sound words as you can in 60 seconds: buzz, crash, pop, sizzle, crunch, splash, hiss... These are called onomatopoeia!

📝 Activity: Three Figurative Language Techniques
  1. Personification: Give human actions to non-human things. "The wind whispered through the trees. The sun smiled down."
  2. Onomatopoeia: Words that imitate sounds. Buzz, crash, sizzle, pop, crunch, splash.
  3. Alliteration: Repeated beginning sounds. "Seven silly sisters sang songs."

Combine all three into one "Mega Poem"!
