Note-Taking Strategies

October Week 3

Age 9

Research Skills
🎯 Learning Goal

Use graphic organizers and simplified Cornell notes to record key information from informational text.

☀️ Warm-Up: Fact Catch

Listen to a 3-minute educational video. Write down 3 facts you remember. Compare notes — did you catch the same facts?

📝 Activity: Learn Two Note-Taking Tools

Tool 1 — Web Organizer: Write the topic in a center circle, then draw lines out to key details.

Tool 2 — Cornell Notes: Fold a paper into two columns. Left (narrower) = keywords or questions. Right (wider) = notes or facts. Notes are NOT full sentences — use abbreviations, symbols, and short phrases.

✏️ Practice: Note-Taking Exercises

Part A: Cornell Notes Practice. Read the passage below and take notes in the Cornell format.

The human body has 206 bones. The smallest bone is in the ear and is called the stapes. The largest bone is the femur in the thigh. Bones protect important organs like the brain and heart. Red blood cells are made inside bones in a soft material called marrow.

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Complete a Cornell Notes page with at least 4 keywords and 4 facts
  • Use abbreviated phrases rather than full sentences
  • Create a Web Organizer with a topic and 4 branches
  • Take notes from an informational passage or video
💡 Easier: Provide a partially filled-in organizer and fill in the blanks.
💡 Harder: Write a 3-sentence summary at the bottom of your Cornell Notes using only your notes.
