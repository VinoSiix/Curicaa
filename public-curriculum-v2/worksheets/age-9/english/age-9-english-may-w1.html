Short Story Writing with Dialogue

May Week 1

Age 9

Creative Writing
🎯 Learning Goal

Write an original short story (3-5 paragraphs) with a clear plot arc, at least 2 characters, and properly formatted dialogue using quotation marks and speaker tags.

☀️ Warm-Up: Story Starter

Pick a prompt: "A girl finds a key in the park that opens a door nobody else can see." Write for 5 minutes without stopping. Then read aloud to build creative fluency!

📝 Activity: Story Mountain Planning

Plan your story using a Story Mountain:

  1. Opening: Introduce character and setting
  2. Build-Up: Events leading to the problem
  3. Climax: The most exciting moment
  4. Resolution: How the problem is solved
  5. Ending: What happens next or how the character feels

Requirements: 3+ paragraphs, 2+ characters, 4+ lines of correctly formatted dialogue.

Part C: Dialogue Rules — True or False?

  1. Start a new paragraph each time a new person speaks.   T / F
  2. Quotation marks go around the speaker tag, not the spoken words.   T / F
  3. Use a comma before the quotation when the speaker tag is at the beginning.   T / F
  4. You can put all dialogue in one long paragraph.   T / F

Part D: Write 4 lines of dialogue between two characters (use correct formatting).

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Write a short story with a complete plot arc
  • Include 2+ characters
  • Write 4+ correctly formatted lines of dialogue
  • Start new paragraphs for new speakers
💡 Easier: Use a provided Story Mountain outline and write 3 paragraphs with 2 lines of dialogue.
💡 Harder: Write a 5-paragraph story with dialogue that reveals character personality.
