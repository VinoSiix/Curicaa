Writing Personal Narratives

September Week 3

Age 9

Narrative Writing
🎯 Learning Goal

Write a focused personal narrative about a real experience with a clear beginning, middle, and end, using sensory details.

☀️ Warm-Up: Small Moment Thinking

Think of a "watermelon" topic (too big, like "My Summer Vacation") vs. a "seed" moment (small, like "The Time I Got Stung by a Bee at the Lake"). Brainstorm 5 seed moments from your life.

📝 Activity: Plan Your Narrative

Use the 3-panel storyboard below. Plan your personal narrative:

  1. Panel 1 — Beginning: Set the scene. Who is there? Where are you? What is happening?
  2. Panel 2 — Middle: The action or problem. What went wrong? What did you feel?
  3. Panel 3 — End: The resolution. How did it end? How did you feel afterward?

Then write your narrative in 3 paragraphs (at least 5 sentences each). Include sensory details: What did you see, hear, smell, feel, taste?

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Write a 3-paragraph personal narrative with a clear sequence
  • Include at least 2 sensory details in the narrative
  • Use a storyboard to plan beginning, middle, and end
  • Write sentences that flow logically from one event to the next
💡 Easier: Use a pre-drawn storyboard and write 1-2 sentences per panel.
💡 Harder: Add dialogue between characters using quotation marks correctly.
