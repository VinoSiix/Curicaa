Comparing Texts: Book vs. Movie

March Week 4

Age 9

Literature Study
🎯 Learning Goal

Compare and contrast a book with its movie adaptation, analyzing what was kept, changed, or added and why those choices were made.

☀️ Warm-Up: Adaptations Brainstorm

List books that have been made into movies. Which ones have you both read AND seen? Good options: Charlotte's Web, The BFG, Matilda, How to Train Your Dragon.

📝 Activity: Book vs. Movie Comparison

Create a 3-column comparison chart: "In the Book Only" | "In Both" | "In the Movie Only." Fill it in while watching and reading. Then discuss: Why did the filmmakers make changes? Write a compare/contrast paragraph with a thesis statement.

Part B: Answer these analysis questions.

  1. What was the biggest difference between the book and the movie?  
  2. Why do you think the filmmakers made that change?  
  3. Was the change good or bad? Why?  
  4. Did the movie capture the theme of the book?  
  5. Which version did you like better? Why?  

Part C: Write a thesis statement comparing the book and movie.

"The movie version of [Title] was [similar to / different from] the book because..."

Part D: Write a compare/contrast paragraph using your chart as evidence.

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Complete a detailed comparison chart with 5+ entries per column
  • Write a paragraph with a thesis statement comparing book and movie
  • Use at least 2 specific examples of differences
  • Explain WHY filmmakers made changes
💡 Easier: Compare a picture book and its short film adaptation.
💡 Harder: Write a full essay arguing whether the book or movie was better, with evidence and a counterargument.
