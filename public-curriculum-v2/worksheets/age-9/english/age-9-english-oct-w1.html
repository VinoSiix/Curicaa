Navigating Informational Text Features

October Week 1

Age 9

Informational Text
🎯 Learning Goal

Identify and use nonfiction text features (headings, captions, diagrams, glossary, index, table of contents) to locate information.

☀️ Warm-Up: Book Pass

Look at 6 different informational books. For each book, find the table of contents, the index, and one diagram in 1 minute. How fast can you find them?

📝 Activity: Text Features Scavenger Hunt

Grab an informational book (like National Geographic Kids or DK Eyewitness). Use the checklist below to find each text feature. Write the page number where you found it.

Part B: Match each text feature to its purpose.

FeaturePurpose
Glossarya) Shows you what a page will look like before you read
Captionb) Lists topics and page numbers at the front of the book
Indexc) Defines important words at the back of the book
Table of Contentsd) Alphabetical list of topics at the back with page numbers
Headinge) Explains what is happening in a photograph
Bold Printf) Tells what a section is about
Diagramg) Shows important vocabulary words
 h) A picture with labels that explains how something works
✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Locate and name at least 8 nonfiction text features
  • Explain the purpose of at least 4 text features
  • Use the index to find a specific topic quickly
  • Use the glossary to find a word's definition
💡 Easier: Focus on the 5 most common features (table of contents, headings, captions, glossary, index).
💡 Harder: Create your own mini informational page that includes at least 5 text features.
