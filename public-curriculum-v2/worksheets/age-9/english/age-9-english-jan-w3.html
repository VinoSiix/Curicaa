🎯 Learning Goal

Use commas in a series, after introductory words, and in dates; use quotation marks for dialogue; use apostrophes for contractions and possessives.

☀️ Warm-Up: Punctuation Police

Find and fix the punctuation errors in these sentences! Use a red pen to "arrest" the mistakes.

📝 Activity: Three Punctuation Stations
  1. Commas: In a series ("apples, bananas, and oranges"), after introductory words ("Finally, we went home."), in dates ("On Monday, January 15, 2025...")
  2. Quotation Marks: Dialogue sandwich — open quote, speaker tag, close quote. ("I love this book," said Maria.)
  3. Apostrophes: Contractions ("don't" = "do not") and possessives ("the dog's bone"). Remember: "it's" always means "it is" — never possession!

Part B: Quotation Marks — Add quotation marks and commas for dialogue.

  1. I want to go home said the little boy.
  2. Where are we going asked Maria.
  3. This is the best day ever shouted Jake.

  5. Did you finish your homework asked the teacher.

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Use commas correctly in a series, after intro words, and in dates
  • Punctuate dialogue with quotation marks and commas
  • Use apostrophes for contractions and possessives
  • Punctuate a 10-sentence passage with no more than 2 errors
💡 Easier: Focus on one punctuation mark per day with a reference card of examples.
💡 Harder: Write a short story paragraph that includes all three punctuation types with no errors.
