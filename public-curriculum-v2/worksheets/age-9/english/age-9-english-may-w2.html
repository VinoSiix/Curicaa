Show Don't Tell Technique

May Week 2

Age 9

Creative Writing
🎯 Learning Goal

Use vivid sensory details and specific actions to 'show' emotions and events rather than simply 'telling' the reader about them.

☀️ Warm-Up: Tell vs. Show

Tell: "Sarah was sad." Show: "Sarah stared at the floor. Her shoulders slumped and a single tear rolled down her cheek as she clutched the crumpled letter." Which version helps you FEEL the sadness?

📝 Activity: Emotion Anchor Chart

Create an Emotion Anchor Chart: for each emotion, brainstorm body language, facial expressions, actions, and internal feelings that SHOW it.

Example — Angry: clenched fists, raised voice, red face, stomping feet. Take 5 "telling" sentences from your story and rewrite them as "showing" sentences.
