Poetry Performance & Analysis

December Week 4

Age 9

Poetry
🎯 Learning Goal

Perform a poem with expression, fluency, and appropriate pacing; analyze a poem for meaning and figurative language.

☀️ Warm-Up: Vocal Tone Practice

Practice saying one poem line in 4 different tones — excited, sad, angry, mysterious. How does changing your tone change the meaning?

📝 Activity: Poetry Performance & Analysis

Select your best poem from this month. Mark it up: underline words to emphasize, draw slash marks for pauses, add notes for volume (loud, soft, whisper). Practice 4 times: alone, in front of a mirror, for a parent, then the final performance.

Part C: Poetry Analysis — Analyze a poem by answering these questions.

"The fog comes
on little cat feet.
It sits looking
over harbor and city
on silent haunches
and then moves on."
— Carl Sandburg, "Fog"

  1. What is this poem about?  
  2. What figurative language is used?  
  3. How does this poem make you feel?  
  4. What is the poet's message?  

Part D: Self-Evaluation — Rate your performance 1-5.

  • Volume (could everyone hear me?):   1 2 3 4 5

  • Pace (was I not too fast, not too slow?):   1 2 3 4 5

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Perform a poem with varied expression and appropriate pacing
  • Use at least 2 dramatic pauses in your performance
  • Analyze a poem for meaning and figurative language
  • Write a 4-sentence analysis of a poem
💡 Easier: Perform a published poem rather than an original, focusing on volume and expression.
💡 Harder: Write a poetry analysis paragraph identifying theme, figurative language, and the poet's purpose.
