Expository Essay Structure

February Week 1

Age 9

Informational Writing
🎯 Learning Goal

Write a structured informational essay with a clear introduction, body paragraphs with topic sentences, and a conclusion.

☀️ Warm-Up: Explain It!

You have 60 seconds to explain how to do something (make a sandwich, ride a bike, play a card game) without skipping steps. Clear explanations need clear structure!

📝 Activity: The Hamburger Model

Use the Hamburger Model for your essay:

  • Top Bun = Introduction: Hook + thesis statement
  • The Meat = Body Paragraphs: Each starts with a topic sentence + 3-4 supporting details
  • Bottom Bun = Conclusion: Restate main idea + final thought

Choose a topic you know well and plan using an outline template. Write the intro together, then body paragraphs independently.

✏️ Practice: Expository Essay

Part A: Topic Sentence Match — Match each topic sentence to 3 supporting details. Cross out the decoy.

Topic Sentences:

  1. Dogs need daily exercise to stay healthy.
  2. Reading every day improves your vocabulary.
  3. The water cycle has four main stages.

Details: a) Dogs should walk at least 30 minutes a day. b) Evaporation turns liquid water into vapor. c) You learn new words from context. d) Running helps dogs burn energy. e) Condensation forms clouds. f) Chapter books have harder words than picture books. g) Dogs can be trained to do tricks. h) Precipitation falls as rain or snow. i) Good readers often have bigger vocabularies.

Part B: Essay Outline — Plan your essay below.

Thesis statement (main idea) 
Body 1 topic sentence

Body 2 details (3) 
Conclusion (restate + end)

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Write a 4-paragraph essay with intro, 2 body paragraphs, and conclusion
  • Each body paragraph has a topic sentence and 3+ supporting details
  • Introduction includes a hook and clear thesis
  • Each paragraph has ONE main idea
💡 Easier: Write 3 paragraphs total with a provided topic sentence frame.
💡 Harder: Write 5 paragraphs with 3 body paragraphs and include a "Did You Know?" fact box.
