Compare & Contrast Writing

February Week 2

Age 9

Informational Writing
🎯 Learning Goal

Write a compare and contrast essay examining similarities and differences between two topics using organized structure.

☀️ Warm-Up: Same or Different?

Grab two similar objects (e.g., an apple and an orange). List 3 ways they're the same and 3 ways they're different in 2 minutes.

📝 Activity: Two Compare/Contrast Structures

Block Method: Paragraph 1 = Topic A completely, Paragraph 2 = Topic B completely, Paragraph 3 = Key similarities and differences.

Point-by-Point: Each paragraph covers one point of comparison (e.g., Paragraph 1: Appearance of both).

Create a Venn Diagram first, then choose your structure. Use signal words: similarly, likewise, in contrast, however, on the other hand, while, both.

✏️ Practice: Compare & Contrast

Part B: Signal Words — Fill in the correct compare/contrast signal word.

  1. Cats and dogs _____ make great pets. (both / however)
  2. Dogs need daily walks. _____, cats are more independent. (Similarly / On the other hand)
  3. Both stories have a happy ending. _____, the characters are very different. (Likewise / However)
  4. Summer is hot and sunny. _____, winter is cold and snowy. (Similarly / In contrast)
  5. Jupiter and Saturn are _____ gas giants. (both / however)

Part C: Write one paragraph comparing or contrasting your two topics. Use at least 2 signal words.

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Create a Venn Diagram with 3+ similarities and 3+ differences
  • Use at least 4 compare/contrast signal words correctly
  • Write a compare/contrast essay using organized structure
  • Use a Venn Diagram as a pre-writing tool
💡 Easier: Use the Block Method with a provided Venn Diagram and sentence starters.
💡 Harder: Use the Point-by-Point Method and include a thesis statement with an overall insight.
