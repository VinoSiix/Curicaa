Multiple Meaning Words & Homophones

April Week 3

Age 9

Vocabulary & Word Study
🎯 Learning Goal

Identify words with multiple meanings (bank, bark, play, light) and homophones (there/their/they're, its/it's, to/too/two) and use them correctly in context.

☀️ Warm-Up: Homophone Riddles

"I'm thinking of a word that sounds like 'flower' but means 'flour.'" "A word that sounds like 'sea' but means 'to look.'" Can you guess each pair?

📝 Activity: Multiple Meanings & Homophones

Multiple Meaning Words: Bank (river bank vs. money bank), bark (tree bark vs. dog sound), play (a game vs. a theater show), light (not heavy vs. brightness).

Top 10 Homophone Pairs: there/their/they're, its/it's, to/too/two, your/you're, hear/here, piece/peace, right/write, flour/flower, bare/bear, whole/hole.

✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Correctly use 8 out of 10 homophone pairs in sentences
  • Identify both meanings of 6 out of 8 multiple meaning words
  • Edit a paragraph with homophone errors
  • Explain the difference between common homophones
💡 Easier: Focus on the 5 most common pairs (there/their/they're, its/it's, to/too/two).
💡 Harder: Study homographs (same spelling, different pronunciation: lead/lead, read/read) and create a quiz.
