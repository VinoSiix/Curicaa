Cause & Effect Writing

February Week 3

Age 9

Informational Writing
🎯 Learning Goal

Identify cause-and-effect relationships in text and write a paragraph explaining why something happened and what resulted from it.

☀️ Warm-Up: Cause & Effect Chain

Start with "I woke up late." Add an effect: "I missed the bus." That becomes a new cause: "I missed the bus, so I had to walk." Keep the chain going for 5 links!

📝 Activity: Cause & Effect Writing

Cause signal words: because, due to, since, the reason, as a result of.

Effect signal words: therefore, consequently, as a result, so, thus, this led to.

Read an informational passage and highlight causes in one color and effects in another. Write a cause-and-effect paragraph using a graphic organizer with arrows.

✏️ Practice: Cause & Effect

Part A: Identify the cause and effect in each sentence. Write C (cause) or E (effect).

  1. Because it rained all day, the garden was flooded.   Cause: _____ Effect: _____
  2. The team practiced every day, so they won the championship.   Cause: _____ Effect: _____
  3. Due to the power outage, we used flashlights.   Cause: _____ Effect: _____
  4. The ice cream melted as a result of the hot sun.   Cause: _____ Effect: _____

Part B: Fill in the signal word to connect cause and effect.

  1. The volcano erupted, _____ ash filled the sky. (so / because)
  2. _____ the heavy snow, school was cancelled. (Therefore / Due to)
  3. She studied hard; _____, she passed the test. (because / consequently)
  4. Plants wilted _____ they weren't watered. (therefore / because)

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Write a cause-and-effect paragraph with a clear topic sentence
  • Include at least 2 cause-and-effect relationships with signal words
  • Use cause/effect signal words correctly
  • Write a conclusion that summarizes the causal chain
💡 Easier: Use a pre-filled graphic organizer with arrows and write sentences from a template.
💡 Harder: Write a multi-paragraph essay analyzing a complex cause-and-effect topic.
