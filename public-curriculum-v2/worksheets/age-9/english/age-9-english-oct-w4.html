Beginning Research with Multiple Sources

October Week 4

Age 9

Research Skills
🎯 Learning Goal

Research a topic using 2-3 different sources, take notes, and write a short informative paragraph.

☀️ Warm-Up: Research Question

Pick a research question: "Why do leaves change color?" "How do volcanoes form?" or "What was life like for children in ancient Egypt?" Share what you already know!

📝 Activity: Guided Research Project

Choose one research question. Gather 2-3 sources (a book, a website like National Geographic Kids, and a video). Take notes from each source using a Web Organizer — one organizer per source. Then combine your notes to write a single informative paragraph (6-8 sentences). Remember to cite your sources: "According to [source name]..."

✍️ Writing Space
✅ Can Your Child...?
  • Write an informative paragraph answering a research question
  • Use facts from at least 2 different sources
  • Include at least one source citation ("According to...")
  • Take organized notes from each source
💡 Easier: Use 2 sources only and write 4-5 sentences with a provided topic sentence.
💡 Harder: Use 3+ sources, include a direct quote with quotation marks, and write 2 paragraphs.
