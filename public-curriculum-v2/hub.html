Zero & Comparing

Ages 5–7Math · Week 3
Warm-up
Read a counting-down book or sing "Five Green and Speckled Frogs" until there are zero left
Main Activity
Spindle Box concept — label cups with 0, 1, 2. Give child popsicle sticks to place the correct amount in each cup. Emphasize that the "0" cup stays empty. Then create two piles of blocks and ask "which has more/less?"
Game / Play
Card War with numbers 1–10 — each player flips a card, the higher number wins both cards
Home Connection
Ask your child to find "zero" things around the house (empty cups, no apples in the fruit bowl) and practice "which pile has more?" with snacks or toys
Assessment
Child correctly identifies empty sets as zero and accurately identifies which of two small groups is larger

Cell Organelles

Ages 13–14Biology · Week 6
Warm-up
"A cell is like a city: the nucleus is city hall, mitochondria are power plants, ribosomes are factories. What else?"
Main Activity
Create an organelle reference chart — nucleus (DNA storage), mitochondria (energy/ATP), chloroplasts (photosynthesis), ribosomes (protein synthesis), ER (transport), Golgi (packaging), lysosomes (recycling). Then build a 3D cell model using household items (jello, fruit, candy, pasta)
Game / Play
Organelle Charades — act out an organelle's function without speaking. Family guesses which one. "I'm the powerhouse!"
Home Connection
Look at an onion skin under a microscope (or watch a cell video online) and identify the organelles you learned about
Assessment
Student correctly identifies and describes the function of 8 organelles and explains the "cell as a city" analogy for each

Our story

Built by a parent,
for every family.

When I started homeschooling my own kids, I couldn't find a curriculum that was complete, affordable, and actually easy to use. Everything was either $1,000+ per child, required hours of prep, or came with expensive lab kits I'd never use.

So I built Curicaa — open-and-go lesson plans from age 5 through SAT prep, using materials you already have at home. Every lesson is laid out week by week so you can focus on teaching, not planning.
